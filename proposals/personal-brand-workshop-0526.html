Part 1 — Step 01

Know Your Why

1
Ask yourself: What transformation do you create for your audience? Not your title. The outcome you produce.
2
Write one sentence that completes this: "I help [who] do [what] so they can [outcome]."
3
Now ask: What life experience or unique path gave you the credibility to say that? That's your edge.
⏸

Pause here. Write your one-sentence why and your unique edge before moving to the next step. You'll need both for your brand brief.

Your answers should look like this
WhyI help early-career professionals navigate corporate culture so they can advance without losing themselves.
EdgeFirst-gen professional who became a VP at 29 with no network and no playbook.

Part 2 — Step 04

Find Your Aesthetic

1
Go to variantly.com in a new tab. This is where we're capturing aesthetic inspiration.
2
Browse the speaker and personal brand site examples. Find a layout or vibe that feels like you.
3
Take a screenshot of the design you like and save it to your desktop.
4
If nothing fits perfectly, describe the vibe in a few words: clean and minimal, bold and colorful, warm and editorial, etc.
Why Variantly? It gives you real examples of what's possible so you're not describing a vibe from scratch. A screenshot is worth a thousand words when prompting a design tool.

Part 2 — Step 06

Review and Iterate

1
When Lovable finishes generating, preview the site using the preview button in the top right.
2
Check: Is your name and title correct? Is your why showing up clearly? Do you have all 3 topics?
3
If something is off, go back to the prompt box and describe the change specifically. Example: "Change the hero headline to say exactly: [your text]"
4
Aim for 2 to 3 rounds of edits maximum. Get it to 80% good. You can always improve it later.

Part 3 — Step 07

Connect Lovable to GitHub

1
If you don't have a GitHub account, go to github.com and create a free one now. Use the same email as your Lovable account.
2
Back in Lovable, click the GitHub button in the top right (looks like a branch icon or says "Connect to GitHub").
3
Authorize Lovable to access your GitHub account when prompted.
4
Lovable will create a new repository. Leave the default name and click confirm.
⏸

Pause here. Wait until you see your repo appear at github.com before moving on. Takes about 30 seconds.

Part 3 — Step 08

Connect GitHub to Netlify

1
Go to netlify.com and create a free account. Use your same email.
2
From your Netlify dashboard, click "Add new site" then "Import an existing project."
3
Choose GitHub as your source. Authorize Netlify to access your GitHub.
4
Select the repo Lovable created. Leave all build settings as default and click "Deploy site."
Netlify → Import Project

Part 3 — Step 09

Your Site Is Live

1
Netlify will give you a URL that looks like random-name-123.netlify.app — that's your live site. Click it.
2
No domain? This Netlify URL is your site. Share it, add it to your LinkedIn bio, and put it in your email signature today.
3
Have a domain? In Netlify go to Site settings → Domain management → Add custom domain. Enter your domain and follow the DNS instructions.
4
Copy your live URL. You're done.
