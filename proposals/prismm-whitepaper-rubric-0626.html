Prismm Research Standard

The white paper evaluation framework.

How to read a model paper, and the bar a strong one has to clear. Use this on every paper you bring back, and carry it into the composite. The goal is not a full table, it is a set of inputs strong enough to build from.

The method

Read every paper twice.

A strong white paper is two things at once, a well-built machine and a substantive argument. Those are different skills, and a paper can be brilliant at one and hollow at the other. So we keep them apart. First pass studies the structure and function with the topic blurred out. Second pass studies the content and whether it earns trust.

Score each paper on both passes. Then, when you build the composite, pull the strongest structural choices from one paper and the strongest content choices from another. Two passes in, two passes out.

Pass 1 · Structure and Function

How the paper is built.

Read it with the topic blurred out. Could you diagram this paper without knowing what it is about? What is each section doing, and how does the whole thing move a reader from the first line to an action?

01

Structure and Logic

Is there a clear arc, or is it a pile of sections? Do the section headers make a claim, or just name a topic?

Can you follow it from a glance at the contents alone?
02

Audience Fit

Is the build aimed at one specific reader, and does every part serve that reader's language and stakes?

Who is this for, and would they feel spoken to?
03

Actionability

Does it convert findings into a clear "so what do I do," or does it stop at describing the situation?

Does each section land on an implication for the reader?
04

Data Storytelling and Visualization

Do the charts and visuals carry the narrative, or just decorate it? Is the same comparison held consistently?

Could you tell the story from the visuals alone?
Pass 2 · Content

What the paper says, and whether it earns trust.

Strip the layout away. Is what is left true, original, and credible? A beautiful structure wrapped around a hollow or self-serving finding is still a weak paper.

05

Original Insight

Is there a non-obvious, ownable finding, or does it just confirm what everyone already assumed? This is the dimension that separates a paper people cite from a paper people skim.

What is the one idea this paper can own?
06

Methodology

Is the data sound, and is the method disclosed? Sample size, who responded, how they were recruited, the limitations. Transparency matters more than scale.

Can you find the base behind every percentage?
07

Source Credibility

Who is behind it, and why should a reader trust them? Named authors, research partners, institutional standing. Note any incentive the publisher has in the findings.

What lends this authority before a single stat is read?
08

Value Delivered

What does the reader actually walk away with? Proof of something, discovery of something new, or just polish with no payoff?

Is this a proof asset, a discovery asset, or filler?
Cross-cutting

Replicability for Prismm

After both passes, one question that draws on everything above. Could we realistically build a version of this with our resources and timeline? What transfers to Prismm and what does not. A paper can be excellent and still be the wrong model to copy, so flag what is liftable and what is out of reach.

How to rate each dimension

Strong, Moderate, Weak.

One word per dimension, with a one-line reason. Apply it the same way every time so the papers are comparable.

Strong

Does this well enough that we should copy it. A clear model for the composite.

Moderate

Present and competent, but not exemplary. Useful with caveats.

Weak

Missing, thin, or a cautionary example of what to avoid.

The deliverable

Build the composite.

The point of this work is not a reference list. It is to study genuinely strong papers from across the finance industry, run both passes on each, and synthesize the best of them into one mock example white paper that shows the quality we are aiming for.

  1. Go wider. Bring at least three more strong papers from distinct sources beyond the immediate cluster. Strong means they hold up on this framework, not just that they are on topic.
  2. Run both passes on each. Structure and function first, content second. Keep the two analyses separate and visible.
  3. Synthesize. Take the strongest structural choices from one paper and the strongest content choices from another, and assemble one composite mock paper.

This does not need to be exhaustively rigorous. It does need to be genuinely good and useful, because the composite sets the credibility bar for Prismm's paper and will directly shape the survey questions we ask.
