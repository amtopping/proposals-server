05 — Risk Scenarios

What to watch and when to move

Scenario A — Sunset moves upstream into pre-death planning

Sunset currently disclaims pre-death planning. If they launch any planning tool — even lightweight — their existing D2C base gives them instant distribution that would take Prismm years to match via institutional channels.

Watch for: Sunset hires a product lead with wealth management background. Blog post or PR framing them as "end-to-end." TOS changes referencing "planning." Series A with fintech-focused lead investor. Their SEC-registered adviser arm expanding scope.

Likelihood: Medium Impact: High
Scenario B — A community bank embeds Sunset as an aftercare benefit

A CU or community bank integrates Sunset into their offboarding workflow — connecting Prismm's target channel to Sunset's product without Prismm in the loop. This is the quietest version of displacement. Now that Prismm is in the Jack Henry network, this risk is more visible but the moat is also stronger.

Watch for: Sunset job postings for enterprise or bank partnership roles. Any press mention of a bank integration. RFP language from target CUs mentioning "post-death settlement."

Likelihood: Low today Impact: Medium
Scenario C — Sunset's funeral director channel expands to bank advisors

Sunset's referral model runs through funeral directors today. If they add financial advisors or bank relationship managers as a referral channel, they begin competing for the same trust relationships Prismm's advisor co-host strategy is building.

Watch for: Sunset attending financial advisor or bank advisor conferences. Any advisor-specific product or partner landing page. Testimonials from bank advisors in their marketing.

Likelihood: Low Impact: Medium

06 — Sales Conversation

When Sunset comes up in a bank pitch

Battlecard

Use when a bank prospect asks about Sunset or estate settlement tools during a Prismm conversation.

What to say
Sunset is a family tool. Prismm is bank infrastructure. They don't compete — they describe what happens when a bank does nothing before a customer dies.
Sunset is reactive. A family opens it after the loss. Prismm is what your institution does before that moment ever arrives.
Every time a family uses Sunset, it's evidence your bank left them without a plan. Prismm closes that gap while the relationship is still yours to own.
Prismm is now in the Jack Henry Fintech Integration Network. If you're running SilverLake, CIF 20/20, or Symitar, we're already compatible with your core.
Why pre-death creates lock-in
Pre-death infrastructure is embedded in the relationship — it's part of how the institution serves the member, not a tool the family finds on their own after death.
Once a bank has a wealth transfer workflow embedded, switching costs are institutional. It's not one family choosing a different app — it's the bank making a platform decision.
Prismm's advisor/attorney co-host channel builds referral relationships Sunset's funeral director model can't replicate at the planning stage.

11 — Bottom Line

The read

Sunset and Prismm are not direct competitors today. They attack different moments — post-death vs. pre-death — and different customers — families vs. institutions. Direct overlap is structurally low.

Sunset has D2C distribution, real public traction, and a content engine that will compound. Prismm just hit its first major institutional proof point with the Jack Henry FIN integration — that's a different kind of momentum, slower to build and harder to displace.

The risk is not Sunset beating Prismm in a head-to-head. The risk is Sunset expanding upstream, or getting embedded in a bank's offboarding workflow, before Prismm has locked in the institutional relationship at that institution. Prismm's online presence gap — stale messaging on external profiles, no content engine, thin LinkedIn presence — means the institutional brand is underdeveloped relative to the product's actual progress.

The opportunity: Prismm can position community banks as the antidote to exactly what Sunset exposes. Families are left scrambling after a loss because their bank gave them nothing before it happened. Prismm is what changes that. And now that it's inside the Jack Henry network, the infrastructure case is real — the narrative just needs to catch up.
