The Revenue Stack

Every offer earns its place by doing one job, and by handing the visitor up to the next rung.

Three tiers, each with a different purpose. Attraction builds trust and turns a reader into a first-time buyer. Core is productized and buys back your time. High-ticket is done-for-you and pays the bills. The point of the stack is not that all three exist. It is that each one passes the right person upward, so nobody finds you, loves the free thing, and then hits a wall with nothing in between to buy.

01
Attraction
Free to $99

Its job is to build trust and earn the first yes. Not to make money. To prove how you think so someone decides you are worth more of theirs.

What lives here
  • Online Presence Report Card — the free tool that shows how you think and routes people in
  • Public Pixels — the ones you build and publish yourself
  • The New Career Project newsletter — feeds the top of the funnel, judged as a feeder, not as revenue

03
High-Ticket
$1,500 to $5,000+

Its job is to pay the bills. Done-for-you, delivered one to one, priced as real work. You do not need many to make good money.

What lives here
  • Contracted Pixels — the ones a business pays you to build for them
  • Full custom automation builds — the systems work
  • Deep consulting engagements — the outreach blitz lands here

Handoffs to watch

Revenue does not leak from missing tiers. It leaks from tiers that do not pass people upward. These are the questions to keep live.

  • Attraction to Core. Does the report card or a public Pixel actually route someone toward a Hubble session or a workshop seat, or does it just sit there as a nice free thing?
  • Core to High-ticket. Does a Hubble session, the Advisor Plan, or a workshop explicitly tee up a full engagement, or does it dead-end as a one-off?
  • The binary trap. Watch for anyone who finds you, loves the free work, and then sees only the expensive door. That is the gap the middle rung exists to close.

Standing notes

Investor identity
Leading with the GP identity on Hubble is the proof, not a misalignment. Deploying capital and backing founders is why someone trusts the advice. It stays as is.
Revisit trigger
The day someone else joins the agency and sells without the investor halo, Hubble's positioning needs its own answer. Until then, hold.
The newsletter
Its home is feeder, not revenue. Never judge it by dollars, because that was never its job in the stack.
