What I actually teach ›
Best practices for AI-assisted coding
I ship production-ready work directly from a chat interface, including live deployments. This page is a live example. But more importantly, I teach teams the conceptual foundations they need to direct AI coding tools effectively, not just prompt them.
The foundation I give teams
  • Browsers can only interpret three things natively: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. If you don't know this, you can't effectively direct AI to build anything that actually runs in a browser
  • Python, for example, cannot render directly in a browser. Understanding what runs where is the prerequisite to building anything real with AI
  • Prompting for code is not typing instructions and hoping. It requires knowing enough about the output to evaluate it and redirect when it goes wrong
  • Most teams I work with go from zero to shipping a working internal tool within one session because the foundation clicks fast once it's framed right
  • This page was built entirely inside Claude with no terminal, no IDE, and no separate deployment workflow

A real client example ›
AI implementation risks
As both a practitioner and an investor, I see AI adoption from two angles. Most failures are not technical. They happen when systems are optimized for efficiency without accounting for how the team actually works or what they actually need to retain.
The problemA client came to me wanting full AI automation across their knowledge workflow. The initial build could do everything: intake, summarize, categorize, draft, and deliver. From a pure efficiency standpoint, it worked.
What we foundThe client had explicitly said their priority was learning retention, encoding knowledge themselves, not just having it processed for them. Full automation was actively working against their goal. The AI was doing so much that they were not absorbing anything.
The redesignWe rebuilt the system so AI functioned as backup and reference, not as primary processor. The client still interacts with material first. The system supports that process rather than replacing it. Same infrastructure, completely different design philosophy.
This is what I mean by building to the team's goals, not just their output. Efficiency is not always the right metric.
