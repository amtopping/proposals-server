Open Decisions: Prismm

Should Prismm Be on Facebook at All?

The institutional B2B buying committee structure and decision-making timeline (months-long) doesn't align with Facebook's impulse-zone strength. Is Facebook simply the wrong room for this buyer profile?

Budget Redirect: Is the Smartest Move to Skip Facebook Entirely?

Campaign Principles: Our Approach

These eight principles guide how we're thinking about each campaign. We want to validate that we're in the right head space.

1

Offer-to-channel fit is decided before the ad. The nature of the offer, not creative or landing page, is the primary driver of whether Facebook can work at all.

2

Consolidate by default. One campaign, one ad set. Don't split cold and warm; Meta blurs targeting anyway, and consolidated data helps it exit learning phase faster.

3

Know your levers. Location, age, gender are hard controls Meta stays inside. Interests, lookalikes, custom audiences are soft hints it will go beyond.

4

Diversify creative inside the one ad set. Test copy with variations feature (multiple headlines and primary texts per ad) rather than fragmenting budget across many ad sets.

5

Unit economics have to survive $30–50 CAC. Fat margin, impulse price band around $50–200, and ideally repeat purchase so lifetime value carries the first sale.

6

Don't advertise the expensive thing directly. Optimize toward a cheap front-end action (booked call, lead, low-ticket intro) and close the real sale downstream.

7

Optimize for quality, not volume. Feed downstream valuable event back to Meta (Conversion Leads, not plain Lead) or you train it to find form-fillers your sales side hates.

8

Be honest about channel fit. The further an offer sits from the ideal profile, the more you must engineer the funnel. At the far end, Facebook may be the wrong room.
