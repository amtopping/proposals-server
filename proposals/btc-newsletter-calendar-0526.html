Issue Function Outlines
JuneThe Margin Revolution9 / 10

What Each Section Does

  • Open: The 52% net burn reduction data. Frame it as the market finally catching up to what disciplined investors always knew.
  • Section 1: Why unit economics matter more than growth narrative in climate tech. The AppHarvest cautionary tale as the counterexample.
  • Section 2: The diverse and women-led teams double. Body of research showing stronger margin and revenue multiples. Performance data, not DEI language.
  • Section 3: What this means for how BTC evaluates companies at entry.

Job Function for Pipeline Investors

  • Establishes BTC's analytical rigor as a portfolio selection filter
  • Signals we are not mission-driven alone — we screen for performance
  • Introduces the portfolio without naming it explicitly
  • Sets up diversity as a returns argument, not a values argument

BTC Tie-In

"This is the lens through which BlackTech Capital has always evaluated entry. The market is now confirming what our portfolio already reflects — that the companies most likely to achieve durable margins are the ones built by teams that have always had to do more with less."

JulyThe Exit Market Is Waking Up9 / 10

What Each Section Does

  • Open: $27.2B exit value in 2025, strongest post-2021 showing. Reframe the headline as a signal, not a stat.
  • Section 1: What late-stage concentration actually means for the full cycle. The relationship between Series C+ valuations and eventual liquidity events.
  • Section 2: The exit pathways available in climate tech — IPOs, strategic acquisitions, secondary markets, future fund buyouts.
  • Section 3: Why early entry in 2025/2026 maps directly to the companies producing those exits.

Job Function for Pipeline Investors

  • Directly addresses the liquidity objection — the most common hesitation in early-stage climate
  • Demonstrates BTC's full-cycle thinking, not just entry discipline
  • Educates less climate-sophisticated investors on how returns materialize
  • Creates urgency — early entry pricing is finite

BTC Tie-In

"The companies we are entering today are the ones that will be acquired by the strategic buyers who are currently going quiet on climate publicly while building their M&A pipelines privately. We invest in the underlying demand, not the narrative."

AugustThe China / International Thesis10 / 10

What Each Section Does

  • Open: China spent $625B on clean energy in 2024. The entire US VC debate is a rounding error by comparison.
  • Section 1: State-level investment. China's 14th Five-Year Plan, India's Hydrogen Mission, Singapore's Future Energy Fund, NEOM. The scale of public commitment outside the US.
  • Section 2: CVC and corporate-level investment. How international corporates are building climate tech supply chains while US corporates go silent.
  • Section 3 (original reporting): Voices from BTC's international investor network.
  • Close: What being an international firm actually means for deal flow, relationships, and returns.

Job Function for Pipeline Investors

  • The single strongest differentiator issue in the calendar
  • Establishes BTC as operating at a global level of analysis US-centric funds cannot match
  • Flaunts international relationships as evidence, not decoration
  • Directly answers: why BTC and not another climate fund
  • Plants the seed for Fund II's international deployment thesis

BTC Tie-In

"Just because the US is going through a psychosis right now does not mean the rest of the world is. BlackTech Capital was built as an international firm from day one. Our portfolio companies are not dependent on US policy tailwinds. Neither is our thesis."

SeptemberThe Divergence Issue8 / 10

What Each Section Does

  • Open: Corporate America mentions of climate on earnings calls down 69%. Physical climate losses hit $417B globally in 2025. Set up the contradiction.
  • Section 1: What corporate America is actually doing vs. what it is saying. The 8% rollback vs. 32% expansion data.
  • Section 2: Corporate Europe as the foil. CSRD, mandatory disclosure, the 40 global jurisdictions adopting ISSB frameworks.
  • Section 3: What this divergence means for buyers of climate tech solutions. The demand is real regardless of the language.

Job Function for Pipeline Investors

  • Resolves what looks like a market contradiction for less sophisticated investors
  • Demonstrates BTC's ability to read signal through noise
  • Reinforces the international positioning from August — corporate Europe is an active buyer
  • Adds texture for investors already warm from prior issues

BTC Tie-In

"The market is sending two signals at once — retreat in the language, acceleration in the physical demand. We invest in the demand, not the language. That distinction is what allows us to find opportunity where others see uncertainty."

OctoberThe National Labs Pipeline8 / 10

What Each Section Does

  • Open: What national labs actually produce. Argonne, NREL, ORNL, Lawrence Berkeley. Decades of federally funded deep science most investors have never thought about as a deal pipeline.
  • Section 1: The technology transfer pathway. How IP moves from lab to license to commercial company. Why this creates defensible moats.
  • Section 2: The current threat. What defunding the federal research infrastructure means for the early-stage climate pipeline over the next decade.
  • Section 3 (live example): Mars Materials. Exclusive license of national lab-derived technology. What that means for defensibility and commercial trajectory.

Job Function for Pipeline Investors

  • Demonstrates BTC's sourcing sophistication — we see pipelines most funds do not know exist
  • Uses a live portfolio company to make the thesis concrete and credible
  • Connects the policy threat to why acting now matters — the pipeline narrows as defunding continues
  • Educates investors on a deal sourcing advantage that is genuinely hard to replicate

BTC Tie-In

"One of our portfolio companies holds an exclusive license on technology that originated inside a federally funded national lab. That pathway — from deep public science to commercial deployment — is one of the most defensible in climate tech. It is also one of the least visible to the broader market. That asymmetry is not accidental. It is how we source."

What Each Section Does

  • Open: The pattern. What the 2009/2010 clean energy vintage produced for funds that deployed during the trough. Draw the macro parallel.
  • Section 1: What skittish capital does to entry pricing. The early-stage gap as a structural pricing inefficiency, not a risk indicator.
  • Section 2: What the companies that survived the last trough had in common. Unit economics. International exposure. Defensible technology.
  • Section 3: Why the window is finite. As exits materialize and capital regains confidence, the entry pricing advantage compresses.

Job Function for Pipeline Investors

  • The closing argument for the investor at the edge of yes
  • Reframes hesitation as a timing risk, not a safety position
  • Synthesizes everything built across the prior five issues into a single investment case
  • Seeds the Fund II conversation by showing BTC's thesis compounds

BTC Tie-In

"The best vintage years in venture are never obvious while you are in them. They become obvious in retrospect, to the people who were not in the room. We are in the room. The question is whether you want to be too."
